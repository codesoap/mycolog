Introduction to mycolog

mycolog helps you keep track of your inventory and notes in mushroom cultivation projects and enables you to easily trace genetics.

Components

This section will explain the components of a mushroom cultivation project, that can be tracked with mycolog and explains some basic processes of mushroom cultivation along the way. To learn more about each of the mentioned methods, I encourage you to consult further resources.

Spores

Spores are the starting point of every mushroom cultivation project and can be taken from fruit bodies of wild or bought specimen or matured grows. Spores typically come either as spore prints or as spore syringes, where the spores are dissolved in water.

Spores can be stored for multiple years for later projects or traded with other cultivators. It is a good idea to frequently take spore prints from your grows.

Mycelium

Mycelium is developed on agar agar dishes or, less commonly, in a nutritious fluid, called a "liquid culture". It is used to create a clean starting point, with which you can proceed to inoculate spawn or, less commonly, grows.

It can be created from different sources: most commonly it is inoculated with spores or with a small cutout of an existing agar agar dish, but it is also possible to use a grain from a spawn or to clone a fruit body onto agar agar.

Mycelium can also be used to store especially interesting genetics long term, in the form of an agar agar slant.

Spawn

Spawn is a sterile, nutritious substrate, into which mycelium, or spores (not recommended) are placed, so that mycelium can colonize it with a minimal chance of contamination. A healthy spawn is the ideal basis for a successful grow. In order to rapidly multiply spawn, it is also common to inoculate new spawn with existing spawn.

Spawn is most commonly some kind of prepared grain, like rye, oats or wheat.

Grows

A grow is the last step of a cultivation project; fruit bodies are developed here. It is most commonly created by mixing fully colonized spawn with some kind of bulk substrate; this process is also called "spawning". Other techniques require you to place spores from a syringe or mycelium directly onto the substrate that will be used for the grow.

Tip: If you decide to store harvested fruit bodies for a while, you can use the token of the grow to label their container. This way you can always check the origin of your yield.

Tokens

Every component is assigned an alphanumeric token. This token is randomly generated and can be used to label your spore prints, agar agar dishes, spawn bags, etc.

Graphs

If Graphviz is installed, you will be able to see graphs at the details pages of all components. These graphs contain all components, that share genetics with the currently selected component. Note that all components in a graph are clickable.
